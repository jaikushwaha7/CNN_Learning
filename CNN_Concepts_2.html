🧠 CNN Mentor: Learn Like You're 5

🧸 What’s the difference between MLP and CNN?

Imagine you’re looking at a picture. A regular neural network (MLP) sees the picture like it’s a really long row of pixels, and it doesn’t know which parts are close together. But a CNN is smarter—it looks at the picture like how we look at it—with eyes that focus on small parts, like a window sliding across.

MLP

MLP flattens the image and connects every pixel to every node.

CNN

CNN uses filters to slide over the image and learn patterns.

🧩 What is Padding?

Padding is like putting a frame around your picture. It helps the filters not miss the edges when they slide.

Padding helps maintain output size or reduce it less aggressively.

👟 What is Stride?

Stride is how far the window jumps as it slides over the image. A stride of 1 means small steps, stride of 2 means big hops!

Bigger stride = faster scanning, but less detail.

🏊 What is Pooling?

Pooling is like picking only the best parts from small pieces of your image. Max pooling takes the biggest number. It helps reduce the size.

Pooling helps make CNNs faster and more efficient by shrinking the data.
